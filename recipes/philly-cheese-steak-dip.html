Philly Cheese Steak Dip

This Philly cheesesteak dip would make a handsome addition to your snack table for the Big Game.

Like all great party foods, it's wonderful hot, warm, room temp, and, I've heard from a reliable

source, even delicious cold.Serve alongside slices baguette. Keep it hot and fresh for guests by baking it 2 batches, or feel free to do this all at once in a 9x13-inch baking dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef top sirloin steaks
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup pepperoncini peppers
  • 7 pickled red peppers(such as Peppadew)
  • 3 jalapeno peppers
  • 2(8 ounce) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 pound provolone cheese0
  • 1/2 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper

Steps to follow

  1. Slice steak into thick pieces. Season generosuly on both sides with salt and pepper

  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over high heat until smoking.Add steak slices and sear until bottoms are browned,

    3 to 4 minutes.Flip over and reduce heat to medium.Cook until juices appear on the tops,3 to 4 minutes more.

    Transfer to a bowl to cool.

  3. Add diced onion, a big pinch of salt, and butter to the meat juices in the pan.Cook and stir over a medium heat,

    scraping up the browned bits, util onions start to soften, 5 to 7 minutes

  4. Dice up the red bell pepper, green bell pepper, pepperoncini, pickled red peppers, and jalapenos until you have 1 or 1/2 to 2 cups.

    Add to the onions; cook and stir until starting to soften, about 5 minutes.

  5. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F(200 degrees C)

  6. Chop steak into small pieces. Place back into the bowl with the accumulated meat juices. Add the onion-pepper mixture and cream cheese.

    Grate in the provolone cheese, saving a little for the top. Drizzle in Worcestershire sauce and sprinkle in cayenne pepper. Mix dip thoroughly

  7. transfer 1/2 of the dip into small baking dish. Smooth the top with a fork. Place baking dish on top of a sheet pan. Sprinkle the remaining

    provolone cheese on top. Save the other 1/2 to bake fresh for your guests.

  8. Bake in the preheated oven until dip is bubbling and heated through, 20 to 25 minutes. Broil until top is browned, about 1 minute more.

    Repeat with the second batch of dip.
